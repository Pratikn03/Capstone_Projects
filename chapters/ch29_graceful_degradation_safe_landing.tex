\chapter{Graceful Degradation and Safe Landing}
\label{ch:graceful-degradation}

This chapter upgrades the battery extension from a single fallback trace to a
four-policy benchmark over prolonged blindness.  The theorem-level object
remains the graceful-degradation result in
Chapter~\ref{ch:temporal-extensions}; the task here is narrower and empirical:
show what the current locked battery benchmark actually says once several
concrete fallback policies are evaluated on one common blackout surface.

\section{Why Prolonged Blindness Matters}

When telemetry reliability~$w_t$ falls below a minimum threshold
$w_{\min}$, the DC3S shield cannot defend arbitrary non-trivial dispatch.
A battery runtime then faces a concrete policy choice:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item continue blindly,
  \item shut down immediately,
  \item ramp down conservatively, or
  \item execute an optimized graceful fallback.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Canonical Benchmark Surface}

The canonical graceful-degradation artifact family is now anchored to one benchmark:
\texttt{reports/paper3/policy\_compare.csv} records the detailed surface over
blackout durations of 4, 8, 12, 24, and 48 hours, while
\texttt{reports/publication/graceful\_four\_policy\_metrics.csv} is the
promoted summary derived directly from that detailed surface.  The four
policies are:

\begin{description}
  \item[Blind persistence.] Continue dispatching with the last trusted control
    even though observation quality has collapsed.
  \item[Immediate shutdown.] Immediately drive action to zero and accept a
    total loss of useful work.
  \item[Simple ramp-down.] Monotonically reduce power toward a benign hold
    using the heuristic taper.
  \item[Optimized graceful.] Use the constructive graceful planner to retain
    as much useful work as possible while still consuming the remaining safe
    horizon.
\end{description}

The detailed benchmark surface sharpens the interpretation of the promoted

graceful retains roughly twice the useful work of the simple ramp-down

\section{Operational Interpretation}

Three battery-facing points matter.

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Immediate shutdown is the zero-work safety floor.}  It remains
    at zero TSVR across the measured blackout window, but preserves no useful
    work.
  \item \textbf{Optimized graceful materially improves on simple ramp-down.}
    On the promoted surface it retains 10.1\,MWh on average versus
    5.05\,MWh for the heuristic ramp-down while matching its zero-TSVR
    performance through the measured blackout window.
  \item \textbf{Graceful fallback dominates blind persistence on the hidden
    safety gap.}  Blind persistence is the only policy that acquires residual
    mean violation rate and severity on the canonical benchmark.
  \item \textbf{Blind persistence is operationally attractive but not the
    defended runtime contract.}  It retains the most useful work, yet it is
    also the only policy that acquires severe residual violation rate and
    severity on the canonical benchmark, and it does not provide the explicit
    certificate-expiry and audit semantics required by the runtime story
    promoted in Chapter~\ref{ch:certos-runtime}.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Bounded Claim}

The current empirical chapter supports a bounded claim:

\begin{itemize}
  \item the repo now contains a canonical four-policy battery benchmark rather
    than a single fallback anecdote,
  \item the promoted publication table now comes from the same benchmark
    surface as the lower-level graceful-degradation artifacts,
  \item optimized graceful fallback retains more useful work than both
    immediate shutdown and the simple ramp-down heuristic while preserving
    zero TSVR on the measured blackout surface, and
  \item blind persistence remains outside the defended runtime contract because
    the canonical benchmark shows residual violation risk at the prolonged
    48-hour frontier, and
  \item the theorem-level dominance claim still lives in
    Chapter~\ref{ch:temporal-extensions}, not in this single measured trace.
\end{itemize}

\section{Summary}

The battery extension is now stronger than a single safe-landing anecdote.
The locked evidence compares four prolonged-blindness policies on one common
benchmark surface and exposes the practical trade-off: immediate shutdown is
the zero-work safety baseline, blind persistence is the uncontrolled failure
mode, and the graceful policies sit between them by keeping bounded useful
work while avoiding measured violations through the 48-hour window.  Within
that bounded surface, optimized graceful improves on the simple ramp-down
heuristic, while blind persistence ceases to be defensible once the blackout
horizon stretches far enough.
