\chapter{Universal Kernel, Contract, and Unification Program}
\label{ch:universality-completeness}

This chapter clarifies what ``universal'' means in the final ORIUS research
program.  It does \emph{not} claim that all six runtime rows already carry
equal proof depth.  Instead, it isolates the reusable theorem kernel for safe
control under degraded observation, states the broader universal program
results under explicit assumptions, and separates those statements from the
current tiered evidence boundary: battery is the reference proof surface,
industrial and healthcare are defended proof-validated instantiations,
autonomous vehicles is proof-candidate, navigation is shadow-synthetic, and
aerospace remains experimental.

The code mirror of that boundary is now explicit.  The canonical production
kernel lives in \path{src/orius/universal_theory/}; the lightweight reference
modules in \path{src/orius/universal/} provide a compact adapter contract,
frontier utilities, and prior-framework reductions used in this chapter.

\section{Observation-Aware Control Is Structurally Necessary (T9)}
\label{sec:t9-universal-impossibility}
% ----------------------------------------------------------------

Theorem~\ref{thm:no-free-safety} shows, on the battery reference surface, that
quality-ignorant safety logic can fail even when observed-state evaluation
looks safe.  The broader universality claim is that this pathology is not a
battery accident; it is a structural consequence of degraded observation.

\begin{theorem}[Universal Impossibility, T9]
\label{thm:universal-impossibility}
Consider a closed-loop controller that does not adapt its safety margin to the
telemetry reliability process $w_t$.  Under persistent observation degradation
with non-zero fault rate and under the standing boundary-reachability
assumptions used throughout this dissertation, there exists a constant
$c > 0$ such that
\[
  \mathbb{E}\!\left[V_T\right] \;\ge\; c \, d \, T,
\]
where $d$ is the mean degradation rate and $V_T$ is the true-state violation
count over the horizon.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Proof (martingale--$\varphi$-mixing argument)]
The proof proceeds in three steps.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Step~1: $\varphi$-mixing fault process.}
By Assumption~A6, the fault injection process $\{F_t\}$ is
$\varphi$-mixing with coefficient $\varphi(k)\to 0$ as $k\to\infty$.
Concretely, $\varphi(k) \le C_\phi\,\rho^k$ for constants $C_\phi > 0$
and $\rho \in (0,1)$, so a buffer gap of $\tau_{\mathrm{mix}}
= \lceil \log(1/\varepsilon)\,/\,|\log\rho| \rceil$ steps makes any
two windows $\varepsilon$-approximately independent in total variation.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Step~2: Separated windows and per-window risk.}
Partition $[1,T]$ into $k$ analysis windows of length $\ell$ separated
by buffer gaps of length $\tau_{\mathrm{mix}}$, choosing
$\ell = \lfloor T\,/\,(2k) \rfloor$ so that $k \le T\,/\,
(2(\ell + \tau_{\mathrm{mix}}))$.  The battery-domain
no-free-safety witness (Theorem~\ref{thm:no-free-safety}) is applied
inside each window: if the controller does not adapt its safety margin
to the OQE signal $w_t$, the observation gap drives the true state
across the constraint boundary while the observed state appears feasible.
The per-window violation probability satisfies
$p_{\mathrm{win}} \ge c\,d$ for a positive constant $c > 0$ that depends
on the boundary-reachability conditions of Assumption~A4.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Step~3: Martingale concentration.}
Define $D_j = Z_j^{\mathrm{win}} - \mathbb{E}\!\bigl[Z_j^{\mathrm{win}}
\mid \mathcal{F}_{(j-1)(\ell+\tau_{\mathrm{mix}})}\bigr]$, where
$Z_j^{\mathrm{win}} \in \{0,1\}$ indicates a violation in window $j$.
The sequence $\{D_j\}$ is a martingale difference sequence with
$|D_j|\le 1$.  By the Azuma--Hoeffding inequality,
\[
  \Pr\!\Bigl[\,\textstyle\sum_{j=1}^k D_j \le -t\,\Bigr]
  \;\le\; \exp\!\Bigl(-\tfrac{2t^2}{k}\Bigr).
\]
Since $\mathbb{E}\bigl[\sum_j Z_j^{\mathrm{win}}\bigr] \ge k\,c\,d$,
setting $t = k\,c\,d/2$ gives
\[
  \Pr\!\Bigl[\,V_T \;\le\; \tfrac{k\,c\,d}{2}\,\Bigr]
  \;\le\;
  \exp\!\Bigl(-\tfrac{k\,c^2\,d^2}{2}\Bigr)
  \;=\;
  \exp\!\Bigl(-\Omega(dT)\Bigr).
\]
Therefore, with probability at least $1 - e^{-\Omega(dT)}$, the
violation count satisfies $V_T \ge \Omega(dT)$.  Taking expectations
confirms $\mathbb{E}[V_T]\ge\Omega(dT)$, completing the proof.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}
The martingale argument is strictly stronger than a Borel--Cantelli
appeal: Borel--Cantelli requires pairwise independence across windows,
which does not hold when the true state carries over between adjacent
windows.  The $\varphi$-mixing approach replaces exact independence with
approximate independence quantified by $\varphi(\tau_{\mathrm{mix}})$;
the buffer gaps absorb the coupling cost without changing the
$\Omega(dT)$ scaling.
\end{remark}

T9 should be read as a \emph{program theorem}: it explains why reliability
conditioning is structurally necessary in any domain where true-state safety
depends on imperfect telemetry.  The defended evidence remains tiered.  Battery
anchors the proof lineage directly; industrial and healthcare support the same
runtime story under defended replay; the weaker rows remain portability
evidence rather than theorem-validating peers.

\section{Reliability-Risk Frontier (T10)}
\label{sec:t10-lower-bound}
% ----------------------------------------------------------------

The core ORIUS battery bound is
\[
  \mathbb{E}\!\left[V_T\right] \;\le\; \alpha(1-\bar{w})T,
\]
where $\alpha$ is the miscoverage level and $\bar{w}$ is the mean reliability.
The universality program asks whether that envelope is merely conservative or
whether it captures the right scaling law.

\begin{theorem}[Information-Theoretic Lower Bound, T10]
\label{thm:information-theoretic-lower-bound}
Under Assumptions A1, A2, A5' (calibration regularity: $g(w_t)\propto(1-w_t)$
near $w_t = 1$), and the conformal coverage condition, any controller $\pi$
faces the lower bound
\[
  \mathbb{E}\!\left[V_T(\pi)\right]
  \;\ge\;
  \tfrac{1}{4}\,\alpha(1-\bar{w})T.
\]
Combined with the T3 upper bound, DC3S is minimax rate-optimal under
degraded observation: $\mathbb{E}[V_T]\in[\tfrac{1}{4}\,\alpha(1-\bar{w})T,\;
\alpha(1-\bar{w})T]$ for any controller.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Proof (Le~Cam two-point method)]
The proof establishes a per-step lower bound on violation probability and
aggregates by linearity of expectation.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Step~1: Two-point hypothesis at step $t$.}
Fix a step $t$ and let $\delta > 0$ be arbitrarily small.  Define two
true states:
$x_{\mathrm{safe}} = \{x : \phi(x) = +\delta\}$ (just inside the
constraint boundary) and $x_{\mathrm{unsafe}} = \{x : \phi(x) = -\delta\}$
(just outside).  A violation occurs at step $t$ if $x_t = x_{\mathrm{unsafe}}$
and the controller dispatches an action as though $x_t$ were safe.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Step~2: TV distance through the degraded channel.}
By Assumption~A2, the observation satisfies
$\|\hat{x}_t - x_t\| \le g(w_t)$ where
$g:[0,1]\to\mathbb{R}_{\ge 0}$ is monotone decreasing with $g(1)=0$.
Under Assumption~A5' (calibration regularity), $g(w_t) \propto (1-w_t)$
near $w_t = 1$.  The two observation distributions
$P_0 = P[\hat{x}_t \mid x_t = x_{\mathrm{safe}}]$ and
$P_1 = P[\hat{x}_t \mid x_t = x_{\mathrm{unsafe}}]$ are uniform balls
of radius $g(w_t)$ centred $2\delta$ apart.  Their total-variation distance is
\[
  \mathrm{TV}(P_0, P_1)
  \;=\; \max\!\bigl(0,\;1 - 2\delta\,/\,g(w_t)\bigr).
\]
As $\delta\to 0$ (the least-favourable boundary states), this collapses to
$\mathrm{TV}(P_0,P_1) \to w_t$ by the calibration regularity condition.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Step~3: Le~Cam's lemma.}
For any decision rule~$\psi$ (the controller's feasibility check),
Le~Cam's lemma gives
\[
  \max\!\bigl(\Pr[\psi\text{ errs}\mid H_0],\;
              \Pr[\psi\text{ errs}\mid H_1]\bigr)
  \;\ge\;
  \tfrac{1-\mathrm{TV}(P_0,P_1)}{2}
  \;\ge\;
  \tfrac{1-w_t}{2}.
\]

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Step~4: Per-step violation probability.}
A violation requires both $H_1$ to be the true hypothesis and the
controller to err.  The prior probability of $H_1$ satisfies
$\Pr[H_1] \ge \alpha/2$, since the conformal coverage condition
implies that at least an $\alpha$ fraction of steps are near the
constraint boundary.  Therefore
\[
  \Pr[Z_t = 1]
  \;\ge\; \Pr[H_1] \cdot \Pr[\psi\text{ errs}\mid H_1]
  \;\ge\; \tfrac{\alpha}{2} \cdot \tfrac{1-w_t}{2}
  \;=\; \tfrac{\alpha(1-w_t)}{4}.
\]

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Step~5: Episode aggregation.}
Summing over $T$ steps by linearity of expectation:
\[
  \mathbb{E}\!\left[V_T\right]
  \;\ge\;
  \sum_{t=1}^T \tfrac{\alpha(1-w_t)}{4}
  \;=\;
  \tfrac{\alpha(1-\bar{w})T}{4}.
\]
This establishes $\mathbb{E}[V_T(\pi)] \ge \tfrac{1}{4}\,\alpha(1-\bar{w})T$
for any controller~$\pi$.  A refined asymmetric two-point construction
tightens the constant from $1/4$ to $1/2$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}
The gap between the lower bound ($\alpha(1-\bar{w})T/4$) and the T3 upper
bound ($\alpha(1-\bar{w})T$) is a factor of 4, which is standard in
Le~Cam minimax lower-bound arguments (see Tsybakov \cite{tsybakov2009introduction},
Chapter~2; Yu~\cite{yu1997assouad}, 1997).  The conclusion is that DC3S is
minimax optimal up to a universal constant: no controller can do
asymptotically better in the degraded-observation regime, and DC3S
achieves the upper bound.  For the thesis, this is the correct precision:
``DC3S is minimax rate-optimal under degraded observation.''
\end{remark}

The resulting \emph{reliability-risk frontier} is useful even when read
conservatively:
\[
  \bar{w}^{\star}
  \;=\;
  1 - \frac{r^{\star}}{\alpha T},
\]
for a target episode-level violation budget $r^{\star}$.  The canonical
runtime helper for the conservative envelope lives in
\path{src/orius/universal_theory/risk_bounds.py}; the richer frontier analysis
used in this chapter is implemented in
\path{src/orius/universal/reliability_safety_frontier.py}.  Together they make
the central design message operational: degraded observation imposes a
quantitative infrastructure requirement, not just a qualitative warning.

\section{Universal Adapter Contract and Typed Kernel (T11)}
\label{subsec:adapter-contract}
% ----------------------------------------------------------------

The final dissertation claim no longer rests on a vague notion of ``adapter
portability.''  The reusable objects are now explicit both in the writing and
in code.

\begin{definition}[Universal adapter contract]
\label{def:adapter-contract}
A domain instantiation is compatible with the ORIUS universality program if it
supplies:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item an observation packet and parsed state;
  \item a reliability assessment $w_t \in [0,1]$;
  \item an observation-consistent state set $U_t$;
  \item a domain safety specification and tightened safe-action set
    $\mathcal{A}_t$;
  \item a repair operator returning a safe action or admissible fallback;
  \item a causal certificate recording the repaired action, uncertainty state,
    reliability state, and assumption tags.
\end{enumerate}
In the simplified reference harness used for theorem miniatures, these objects
collapse to four adapter operations:
\texttt{observe()}, \texttt{uncertainty\_set()}, \texttt{repair()}, and
\texttt{fallback()}.
\end{definition}

The canonical typed implementation is provided by
\path{src/orius/universal_theory/contracts.py} and
\path{src/orius/universal_theory/kernel.py}.  The smaller reference harness in
\path{src/orius/universal/contract.py} packages the same idea into a compact
machine-checkable contract with five invariants:
\begin{description}
  \item[\textbf{Inv 1} Repair membership.] The repaired action lies in the
    tightened safe-action set.
  \item[\textbf{Inv 2} Monotone tightening.] Lower reliability never enlarges
    the admissible set.
  \item[\textbf{Inv 3} Reliability boundedness.] Reliability remains in
    $[0,1]$.
  \item[\textbf{Inv 4} Calibration consistency.] The inflation law preserves
    the base conformal surface at full reliability.
  \item[\textbf{Inv 5} Failure completeness.] Total observation failure
    collapses the admissible action set to the fallback regime.
\end{description}

\begin{theorem}[Universality template, T11]
\label{thm:universality-completeness}
If a domain instantiation satisfies the universal adapter contract and its
domain-specific proof obligations instantiate the same repair-membership,
monotone-tightening, and fallback admissibility arguments used on the battery
reference surface, then the ORIUS degraded-observation kernel yields the same
one-step safety-preservation structure and the same episode-level
$\alpha(1-\bar{w})T$ envelope.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
The battery proof ladder does not rely on battery nomenclature directly; it
relies on a smaller set of structural facts.  The repair operator must return
an action inside the tightened set, the tightened set must contract as
reliability falls, and the fallback must remain admissible when the safe set
collapses.  Once those obligations are instantiated in a new domain, the same
one-step safety-preservation argument applies, and summing over time produces
the same degradation-sensitive episode envelope.  The theorem therefore
functions as a \emph{template theorem}: universality comes from matching the
structural proof obligations, not from asserting that every domain already
inherits the battery proof automatically.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Machine-Checkable Contract Surfaces}

Two levels of executable checking now exist in the repository.

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Production structural contract.}
    \path{src/orius/universal_theory/contracts.py} exposes the typed
    \texttt{DomainInstantiation} protocol together with a lightweight
    structural verifier.  The focused regression test
    \path{tests/test_universal_theory.py} checks canonical battery,
    industrial, healthcare, and vehicle adapters against that surface and then
    exercises the new universal step kernel end-to-end.
  \item \textbf{Reference invariant harness.}
    \path{src/orius/universal/contract.py} contains a more explicit invariant
    checker used for theorem miniatures and reductions.  The focused tests
    \path{tests/test_universal_contract.py} and
    \path{tests/test_unification.py} deliberately construct compliant and
    broken adapters to show which invariants matter.
\end{enumerate}

The simplified usage pattern is:

\begin{verbatim}
from orius.universal.contract import ContractVerifier

verifier = ContractVerifier(adapter, alpha=0.05)
verifier.check(z_t=np.array([0.0]), q_t=0.1)
\end{verbatim}

That harness is intentionally modest.  It does not replace the defended
domain-specific artifacts.  It makes the universality program executable: the
reader can see the exact invariants, violate them, and observe the resulting
contract failure.

\section{Unification: ORIUS Subsumes Prior Frameworks}
\label{sec:unification}
% ----------------------------------------------------------------

\begin{theorem}[Unification]
\label{thm:unification}
The following prior safe-control families can be represented as special cases
of the ORIUS degraded-observation kernel:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Control Barrier Functions (CBF).} A fixed reliability policy
    $w_t \equiv 1$ together with barrier-defined action repair.
  \item \textbf{Robust MPC.} A constant reliability policy induced by a fixed
    tube radius together with projection onto the tube-tightened feasible set.
  \item \textbf{Safe RL.} A learned or posterior-derived reliability policy and
    a learned uncertainty surface coupled to a safety repair operator.
\end{enumerate}
ORIUS strictly generalises these reductions by allowing the reliability signal
to vary with the actual observation channel and by attaching an explicit
certificate surface to each repaired action.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
The repository contains executable reference reductions in
\path{src/orius/universal/unification.py}.  \texttt{CBFAsORIUS} fixes
$w_t \equiv 1$ and uses barrier-style projection.  \texttt{RobustMPCAsORIUS}
fixes $w_t$ to the normalised tube radius and uses tube-style tightening.
These are not production replacements for full CBF or MPC solvers; they are
small reduction witnesses showing that ORIUS can express both within one
degraded-observation contract.  Safe RL fits the same pattern once posterior
concentration or uncertainty drives the reliability term.
\end{proof}

\section{Constraint-Class Mismatch: When 1-D Action Repair Is Insufficient}
\label{sec:constraint-class-mismatch}
% ----------------------------------------------------------------

The DC3S shield project each candidate action onto a tightened interval
derived from the conformal uncertainty set.  This is sufficient whenever the
domain safety constraint is a box---or more generally, whenever it decomposes
as a monotone function of a single action coordinate.  The following
proposition shows that velocity-dependent constraints, such as the headway
constraint in the autonomous-vehicle domain, require a strictly larger repair
surface.

\begin{proposition}[Constraint-class mismatch barrier]
\label{prop:constraint-class-mismatch}
Let the true safety constraint be the velocity-dependent headway condition
\[
  \phi(x_t)
  \;=\;
  \bigl[\,\mathrm{gap}_t \;\ge\; d_{\min} + \tau\,v_t\,\bigr],
  \quad d_{\min},\tau > 0,
\]
where $x_t = (\mathrm{gap}_t, v_t)$ and the gap obeys
$\mathrm{gap}_{t+1} = \mathrm{gap}_t + \Delta t\,(v_{\mathrm{lead}}
- v_t - a_t\,\Delta t)$.  Suppose the DC3S shield projects the
candidate acceleration into a one-dimensional safe set
\[
  \mathcal{A}_t
  \;=\;
  \bigl[\,a_{\min},\;
    (b_t - v_t)/\Delta t\,\bigr],
  \quad b_t = \mathrm{gap}_t\,/\,(\Delta t + \tau) \in \mathbb{R},
\]
and suppose that there exist states $(\mathrm{gap}_t, v_t)$ with
$v_t > (\mathrm{gap}_t - d_{\min})\,/\,\tau$---that is, $x_t\notin\mathcal{S}$
already at the start of the step.
Then:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item No action $a_t \in [a_{\min}, a_{\max}]$ moves $x_{t+1}$ into
    $\mathcal{S}$ in one step, because the constraint violation is
    determined by \emph{current} $(v_t, \mathrm{gap}_t)$, not by the
    next-step speed the shield controls.
  \item The shield's one-dimensional repair is therefore infeasible for
    current-step constraint satisfaction: $\phi(x_t)$ depends on the
    pre-action state, not on the post-action state.
  \item DC3S's T3 upper bound $\mathbb{E}[V_T]\le\alpha(1-\bar{w})T$
    remains valid for the \emph{next-step} constraint (the set the shield
    can actually control), but it does not apply to the \emph{current-step}
    plant-level constraint that the AV evaluation harness measures.
\end{enumerate}
Consequently, the AV domain incurs a structural proof gap: the shield repairs
future-step feasibility, but the plant reports current-step violations.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
If $v_t > (\mathrm{gap}_t - d_{\min})\,/\,\tau$, then
$\mathrm{gap}_t < d_{\min} + \tau v_t$, so $\phi(x_t) = \mathrm{False}$
regardless of $a_t$.  The action $a_t$ affects $v_{t+1} = v_t + a_t\,\Delta t$
and $\mathrm{gap}_{t+1}$, but not the evaluation $\phi(x_t)$.
The shield's admissible set $\mathcal{A}_t$ therefore provides no mechanism
to avoid the step-$t$ violation; it only constrains step-$(t+1)$.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[AV promotion requirements]
\label{cor:av-promotion}
Promotion of the autonomous-vehicle domain from proof-candidate to
proof-validated requires at least one of the following:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\alph*)]
  \item \emph{Augmented state repair}: the shield solves a two-dimensional
    quadratic program over $(a, v)$ that explicitly enforces
    $\mathrm{gap}_t \ge d_{\min} + \tau v_t$ as a current-state feasibility
    constraint, activating a full-brake fallback when the state is already
    outside~$\mathcal{S}$.
  \item \emph{TTC reformulation}: replace the headway constraint with a
    time-to-collision lower bound, $\mathrm{TTC}_t \ge t_{\min}$, whose
    gradient with respect to $a_t$ is non-zero and admits a valid
    one-dimensional repair.
  \item \emph{Predictive entry barrier}: add a one-step lookahead check
    that refuses to enter states from which $\phi(x_{t+1}) = \mathrm{False}$
    is unavoidable, reducing the observed TSVR to the T3 envelope.
\end{enumerate}
Until one of these is in place, the 2.78\% TSVR in the AV row is a
\emph{structural} residual, not a conformal-coverage residual, and the domain
correctly remains at the proof-candidate tier.
\end{corollary}

\section{Chapter Summary}
\label{sec:ch37-summary}
% ----------------------------------------------------------------

This chapter finishes the project at the right level of ambition.

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{T9} frames observation-aware safety logic as structurally
    necessary under persistent degradation.
  \item \textbf{T10} turns mean reliability into a quantitative
    infrastructure-to-risk frontier.
  \item \textbf{T11} packages universality as a typed kernel and adapter
    template, not as a claim that every domain already has battery-level proof
    closure.
  \item \textbf{The unification theorem} explains how prior safe-control
    families sit inside the same degraded-observation framework.
\end{enumerate}

The result is a sharper and more defensible universal claim: ORIUS is a
domain-agnostic theory-and-software kernel for safe control under degraded
observation, with tiered cross-domain evidence and a battery-anchored proof
surface, rather than an assertion of equal guarantees across all evaluated
domains.
