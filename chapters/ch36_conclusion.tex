\chapter{Conclusion}
\label{ch:conclusion}

The central finding of this thesis is concrete: when telemetry degrades,
a controller can satisfy its observed constraints while violating the true
physical envelope of the system.  This hidden gap---the
Observation-Action Safety Gap (OASG)---is measurable, operationally
meaningful, and correctable.  ORIUS proves this across five distinct
cyber-physical systems.  The battery domain provides the formally locked
reference surface, four additional non-battery domains provide
replay-depth empirical validation, and navigation adds simulation-backed
portability evidence.

\section{Universal Framework Outcome}

The ORIUS framework demonstrates that the OASG phenomenon is not a
battery-specific artifact.  It is a structural consequence of any
closed-loop controller that optimises against degraded telemetry.  The
five-stage DC3S pipeline---Detect, Calibrate, Constrain, Shield,
Certify---operates on domain-abstract objects (observation vector,
reliability score, conformal region, feasible set, projected action) and
therefore transfers to any CPS for which a \texttt{DomainAdapter} can be
implemented.

\section{Domain Coverage and Empirical Results}

Five defended cyber-physical domains were validated under a unified fault schedule
(dropout~15\%, spike~8\%, stale~10\%) at $N = 2000$ fault-stressed steps:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Battery} (reference): 3.9\% baseline TSVR $\rightarrow$
    0.0\% under DC3S.  Full theorem-to-code-to-artifact lineage
    established in Parts~I--II.
  \item \textbf{Autonomous Vehicles}: 3.65\% $\rightarrow$ 0.0\%
    (99.6\% repair rate, mean $\bar{w}_t = 0.768$).
  \item \textbf{Industrial Process Control}: 21.7\% $\rightarrow$ 0.0\%
    (90.7\% repair rate, mean $\bar{w}_t = 0.660$).
  \item \textbf{Medical Monitoring (ICU Vitals)}: 7.55\% $\rightarrow$ 0.0\%
    (37.2\% repair rate, mean $\bar{w}_t = 0.812$).
  \item \textbf{Aerospace (Flight Envelope)}: 26.65\% $\rightarrow$ 0.0\%
    (77.3\% repair rate, mean $\bar{w}_t = 0.782$).
\end{itemize}

Navigation remains part of the broader ORIUS portability package, but it is
simulation-backed rather than part of the defended five-domain equivalent-depth
claim: 25.8\% $\rightarrow$ 9.25\% (100\% repair rate, mean $\bar{w}_t = 0.773$).
All five defended domains show DC3S reducing true-state violations by a factor
of ten or more.  Pipeline latency remains sub-millisecond across the defended
five-domain set (P95~$<$~0.1~ms), confirming ORIUS is suitable for
fast-control-loop applications.

\section{Formal Apparatus and Theorem Ladder Scope}

The full formal apparatus of this dissertation consists of 18 theorems,
5 lemmas, 7 propositions, 10 corollaries, 11 definitions, and 16 standing
assumptions---67 formal items in total.  Two foundational theorems are
established in Part~I before the formal ladder:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{S1} (Theorem: Existence of the Illusion under Dropout,
    Chapter~\ref{ch:safety-illusion}): proves that a
    controller can satisfy observed constraints while violating true
    physical safety under any positive dropout rate.
  \item \textbf{S2} (Theorem: Informal Safety Guarantee of DC3S,
    Chapter~\ref{ch:safety-illusion}): establishes the
    informal correctness guarantee that motivates the DC3S design.
\end{itemize}

The eight principal theorems of the formal DC3S ladder (T1--T8) are then
stated, proved, and connected to code artifacts in
Chapters~\ref{ch:theorem-oasg}--\ref{ch:temporal-extensions}.
The Domain Instantiation subsections in each of those chapters demonstrate
that no battery-specific assumption is required: each abstract object
($z_t$, $w_t$, $C_t^{\text{RAC}}$, $\mathcal{A}_t$, $a_t^{\text{safe}}$,
$v(\cdot)$) maps directly to its domain-specific counterpart in AV,
industrial, healthcare, and aerospace.  All 18 theorems pass the integrated
theorem gate with locked code anchors and artifact traceability
(see \texttt{reports/publication/integrated\_theorem\_gate.csv}).

\section{Final Boundary}

This thesis does not claim that ORIUS is ready for deployment in any
safety-critical domain without domain-specific validation.  It claims
that:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item The OASG phenomenon is real and measurable in any CPS with
    degraded telemetry.
  \item The DC3S architecture provides a principled correction mechanism
    that is domain-agnostic by construction.
  \item Empirical validation across the defended five-domain set confirms
    equivalent safety performance under a shared fault schedule.
  \item The battery domain provides the complete reference instantiation
    with full theorem-to-artifact lineage; the four non-battery defended
    domains provide equivalent replay-depth empirical validation under the
    shared universal harness, and navigation provides simulation-backed
    portability evidence.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Closing Statement}

The ORIUS thesis ends where a rigorous framework contribution should: with
a locked, traceable, battery-anchored safety package defended across five
domains, extended by simulation-backed navigation portability evidence,
and ready for deeper domain-specific validation at each deployment site.
