\begin{abstract}
ORIUS treats degraded observation as a universal action-semantics hazard for Physical
AI: a controller may act legally on the observed state while becoming unsafe on the
true state. We present ORIUS, Observation--Reality Integrity for Universal Safety, as a
typed runtime layer that detects degraded telemetry, calibrates the
observation-consistent state set, constrains the admissible action set, repairs or
replaces unsafe candidate actions, and emits auditable certificates before actuation.
The paper is universal-first rather than battery-origin. It combines one runtime kernel,
one benchmark schema, one governance surface, one theorem bridge, and three defended
program rows spanning battery energy storage, autonomous vehicles, and healthcare
monitoring. The evidence posture is explicit: battery is the witness row, while
autonomous vehicles and healthcare monitoring are defended bounded rows. The resulting
claim is architectural universality under one runtime contract and one governed
three-domain evidence ladder, not flat empirical closure across every conceivable domain.
\end{abstract}
