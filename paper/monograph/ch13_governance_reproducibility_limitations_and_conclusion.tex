\chapter{Governance, Reproducibility, Limitations, and Conclusion}
\label{ch:governance-reproducibility-limitations-conclusion}

\section{Limitations and blocked rows}
The dissertation's strongest limitation is empirical, not conceptual. The runtime grammar is
broader than the deepest currently defended evidence. Battery remains the witness row with
the most complete theorem-to-code-to-artifact chain. Industrial process control and
healthcare monitoring support defended bounded interpretations. The autonomous-vehicle row is
a defended bounded runtime row under the current artifact surface, while canonical raw-data
closure remains incomplete. Navigation remains blocked by the absence of a defended
real-data train--replay chain. Aerospace remains blocked by the absence of a defended
multi-flight runtime validation surface with material post-repair gain.

Those limits matter because ORIUS is not presented here as a universal optimal controller or
as a finished equal-domain deployment program. It is presented as a runtime safety layer
whose strongest current evidence is deep in one witness domain and bounded across several
others. The open rows should therefore be read as the remaining empirical work required to
extend the thesis, not as defects that invalidate the defended core.

\section{Reproducibility and evidence boundary}
The manuscript keeps every headline claim tied to tracked figures, tables, manifests, or
claim matrices so that the defended surface remains auditable after the fact. The purpose of
that discipline is simple: a universal safety thesis should be inspectable at the level of
artifacts, not only persuasive at the level of prose. In that exact sense, the dissertation
is universal in architecture, tiered in evidence.

The evidence boundary is therefore factual rather than rhetorical. Claims backed by theorem
statements remain theorem claims. Claims backed by replay, runtime traces, and governed
artifacts remain validated claims. Claims that depend on future real-data closure for
navigation or multi-flight aerospace closure remain open research steps and are not promoted
into defended headline language.

\section{Conclusion}
ORIUS establishes one durable idea for physical AI: degraded observation should be treated
as a first-class runtime safety problem rather than as a residual modeling nuisance.
Whenever observed-state legality and true-state legality can diverge, safe release requires
more than nominal intelligence. It requires explicit reliability estimation, conservative
state widening, repaired action or bounded fallback, and certificate-aware release.

The mechanism that makes that claim concrete is explicit in the dissertation. A
\texttt{DomainAdapter} exposes the domain safety predicate and fallback object. The
universal benchmark schema exposes one replay contract for truth, observation, intervention,
fallback, certificate validity, and latency. CertOS exposes the lifecycle semantics that
determine when repaired action may still be released. Together, those three surfaces are the
implementation backbone of ORIUS and the reason this thesis is more than a conceptual safety
manifesto.

ORIUS therefore does not ask every domain to share a controller, a plant model, or an
optimization objective. It asks every domain to make the release boundary explicit. By doing
so, it turns true-state violation, intervention burden, fallback usage, certificate
validity, and runtime latency into scientific objects that can be compared across domains
without pretending the domains themselves are identical.

The thesis therefore closes on a claim that is both ambitious and disciplined. ORIUS already
supports a reusable runtime safety layer for physical AI under degraded observation. It does
not yet support the stronger claim that every supported domain is equally mature. That
boundary should be treated as a scientific virtue rather than a rhetorical compromise:
structural universality is defended now, while equal-domain closure remains an empirical
program that must be earned.

If physical AI is to move beyond domain-by-domain reactive patching, it needs a common
runtime discipline for deciding what remains safe when the observation channel begins to
fail. ORIUS provides that discipline in a form that is mathematical enough to be analyzed,
empirical enough to be tested, and operational enough to govern release. That is the thesis
claim this dissertation defends.
