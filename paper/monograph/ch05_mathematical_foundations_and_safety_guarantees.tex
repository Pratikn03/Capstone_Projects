\section{Assumptions and theorem discipline}
The dissertation keeps the theorem surface intentionally explicit. Every strong guarantee
depends on a named assumption set, and those assumptions are recorded in the assumption
register in Appendix~\ref{app:assumptions}. The point of the theory chapter is not to
overclaim generality. It is to show exactly which parts of the ORIUS argument are proved,
which are validated empirically, and which remain bounded by current evidence.

\section{The theorem ladder}
The current ORIUS theorem ladder establishes four core ideas:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item OASG can arise even when observed-state legality appears intact.
  \item Reliability-aware repair preserves safety under stated uncertainty and feasibility
  assumptions.
  \item The risk envelope tightens as reliability degrades, so intervention pressure is a
  lawful consequence of degraded observation rather than an arbitrary heuristic.
  \item There is no free safety: quality-ignorant control can be driven into true-state
  violation by admissible degradation regimes.
\end{enumerate}
The deepest theorem-to-code-to-artifact chain remains battery-backed, but the theorem
objects themselves are written against universal typed contracts and runtime objects.

\section{Risk envelope interpretation}
ORIUS does not prove safety from a single forecast accuracy number. The risk envelope is
expressed in terms of uncertainty coverage, miscoverage, and mean reliability. That is why
the dissertation can connect theorem statements to runtime calibration and certificate
semantics without collapsing them into a purely statistical paper or a purely control
paper.

The dissertation repeatedly returns to one discipline rule: theorem statements must point
to explicit assumptions, typed runtime objects, and tracked empirical diagnostics. That is
why the proof surface remains durable even when empirical maturity differs across domains.

\section{Proved, validated, and roadmap claims}
The theory chapter is also the place where the dissertation's claim boundary becomes
reader-visible. Proved claims live here and in the proof appendix. Validated claims are
only promoted when replay, calibration, runtime, and governance artifacts exist. Roadmap
claims remain in bounded future-work language. This separation is the mechanism that lets
ORIUS argue for a universal safety layer without pretending that every supported row is
already equal in empirical closure.
