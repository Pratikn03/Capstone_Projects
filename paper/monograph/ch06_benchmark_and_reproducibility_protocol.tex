\chapter{Benchmark and Reproducibility Protocol}
\label{ch:benchmark-reproducibility-protocol}

\section{Dual-trajectory evaluation as the core benchmark principle}
ORIUS-Bench is built around dual-trajectory replay. Every evaluation episode distinguishes
between what the runtime \emph{observes} and what the plant or replay harness treats as
the \emph{true} safety surface. This is the only reliable way to measure OASG, because
observed-state legality alone cannot reveal whether the action was physically safe.

\section{Replay, faults, and deterministic schedules}
The benchmark uses deterministic fault schedules so that replay, comparison, and
certificate traces remain reproducible. Fault families include dropout, stale holds,
delay, reordering, spikes, drift, and cross-domain analogs. The important scientific
point is not merely that faults exist; it is that they can be replayed against the same
true-vs-observed contract across domains.

\section{ORIUS-Bench metrics}
The dissertation evaluates ORIUS through a metric family that matches the runtime object:
true-state violation rate (TSVR), observation-action safety gap, certificate validity,
graceful degradation quality, intervention burden, audit completeness, and runtime
latency. These metrics prevent the dissertation from drifting into a pure forecasting or
pure control performance narrative.

\section{Artifact provenance and claim locking}
The benchmark protocol is inseparable from the claim-lock pipeline. The canonical thesis
surface is \texttt{paper/paper.tex}, but its headline tables and figures are backed by
manifests, publication matrices, and governed artifact paths. This means the dissertation
is not ``about'' results in the abstract; it is a storyboard of verifiable evidence.

\section{Strict universal mode}
For the dissertation, benchmark discipline also means refusing battery-shaped shortcuts in
universal language. Domain-neutral replay predicates, adapter hooks, and parity gating are
the canonical surfaces. Legacy compatibility fields can remain for historical or bounded
support use, but they are not allowed to define the universal benchmark story.
