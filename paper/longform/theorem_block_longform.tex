\paragraph{From battery witness to universal ORIUS layers.}
The theorem ladder above is the locked battery proof witness anchored by
T1--T8.  The same scaffold supports the defended ORIUS extension layers:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,topsep=2pt,itemsep=1pt]
  \item \textbf{Temporal validity:} takes the certificate horizon
        theorem (T5) and the expiration bound (T6) and turns them into a
        runtime validity-horizon engine
        (\texttt{dc3s/half\_life.py}, \texttt{dc3s/reachability.py}).
  \item \textbf{Graceful fallback:} takes the fallback existence
        theorem (T7) and the zero-dispatch safe-hold and builds a constructive
        four-policy degradation planner (\texttt{dc3s/graceful.py}).
  \item \textbf{Benchmark discipline:} formalizes the evaluation
        contract that the battery witness depends on into a replayable,
        schema-governed ORIUS-Bench (\texttt{orius\_bench/}).
  \item \textbf{Bounded composition:} shows that the local certificate
        guarantee from the battery ladder does \emph{not} compose under shared
        feeder constraints, and provides centralized and distributed repair
        protocols (\texttt{multi\_agent/}).
  \item \textbf{Runtime governance:} wraps the full certificate
        lifecycle in a CertOS runtime with three invariants (INV-1, INV-2,
        INV-3) and an auditable hash chain (\texttt{certos/}).
\end{itemize}
Each extension stays bounded: temporal validity and graceful fallback remain
theorem-backed within the battery witness, benchmark discipline fixes the
evaluation contract, bounded composition remains a counterexample-plus-repair
surface, and runtime governance remains a bounded lifecycle layer.  They do
not upgrade bounded evidence into equal-domain theorem closure.
