\begin{abstract}
DC³S addresses battery dispatch under unreliable IoT telemetry by coupling telemetry-reliability scoring, conformal interval inflation, and safety-constrained action repair in one online loop. In the \texttt{drift\_combo} CPSBench scenario, DC³S raises mean PICP@90 to 38.3\% from 33.8\% for deterministic dispatch, while holding intervention rate at 0.00\%. This calibration gain is achieved with wider mean intervals (2249.64 vs 666.98) and an expected cost of USD 61,310,572.02, which is USD 160,899.55 above \texttt{robust\_fixed\_interval}. The resulting tradeoff is explicit rather than hidden: when telemetry quality degrades, DC³S buys coverage and eliminates observed violation events by widening uncertainty sets and repairing actions against safety constraints.

This paper presents DC³S as a telemetry-reliability-weighted conformal safety shield for battery dispatch under sensor faults such as dropout, delay jitter, stale readings, out-of-order packets, and spikes. The method combines online fault scoring, drift-aware uncertainty inflation, dispatch optimization, and certificate-backed audit persistence within the GridPulse stack. The contribution is not a new battery objective alone, but a calibrated control wrapper that converts degraded telemetry into bounded uncertainty and executable safe actions. Publication-facing quantitative claims remain locked to repository artifacts, run IDs, and validator checks so manuscript updates do not drift from source data. The result is a decision-system framing in which calibration, cost, intervention behavior, and governance are evaluated together under faulted operating conditions.
\end{abstract}

\noindent\textbf{Keywords:} Machine Learning, Energy Forecasting, Battery Dispatch, Robust Optimization, Conformal Prediction, Stochastic Programming, MLOps, Grid Operations

\section{Introduction}

\subsection{Motivation: autonomy + telemetry dependence}
Prediction-only systems do not deliver operational action. Grid operators need feasible dispatch decisions under uncertainty while balancing cost and carbon objectives. GridPulse is designed as a closed decision loop:
\[
\text{Forecast} \rightarrow \text{Optimize} \rightarrow \text{Dispatch} \rightarrow \text{Measure} \rightarrow \text{Monitor}
\]

\subsection{Failure mode: telemetry degradation \texorpdfstring{$\rightarrow$}{->} miscalibrated uncertainty \texorpdfstring{$\rightarrow$}{->} unsafe dispatch}
The central problem is integrated decision quality rather than isolated forecast error. The system must jointly satisfy five requirements: accurate short-horizon forecasting, uncertainty quantification usable in optimization, physically feasible dispatch, measurable impact vs. baselines, and reproducible claim traceability across manuscript formats.

\subsection{Scientific novelty beyond integration}
Most prior work treats forecasting, uncertainty, dispatch, and reporting as loosely coupled stages. This thesis makes three method-level claims beyond engineering integration:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{RAC-Cert as a control-coupled conformal method:} interval width is adapted by telemetry reliability, drift state, and dispatch sensitivity, then enforced through shielded repair and certificate persistence, coupling uncertainty construction directly to control risk.
    \item \textbf{Truth-vs-observed safety semantics:} safety is evaluated on \(SOC^{true}\) while control actions are chosen from degraded \(SOC^{obs}\), exposing hidden violation risk under telemetry faults that is masked by observed-state-only evaluation.
    \item \textbf{Governance as an experimental object:} claim validity is machine-checkable through manifest-locked sources, claim-level provenance, and validator gates, making reproducibility a testable release condition rather than a narrative claim.
\end{enumerate}
These novelty claims are evaluated only within the locked DE/US evidence scope and are not asserted as universal guarantees across all grids.

\subsubsection*{Metric Interpretation}
Primary comparison metrics are RMSE, MAE, sMAPE, and $R^2$. MAPE is recorded but can be numerically unstable in low-denominator regimes (notably solar nighttime and near-zero wind segments), so interpretation prioritizes sMAPE and absolute error metrics.
Interpretation policy:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
    \item RMSE and MAE are primary for magnitude-sensitive operational quality.
    \item sMAPE supports relative comparability across differently scaled targets.
    \item $R^2$ is retained as fit context, not a standalone decision metric.
    \item MAPE is treated as supplementary in near-zero target regimes.
\end{enumerate}

\subsubsection*{Forecast Inference Contract}
API-level forecast inference (\path{services/api/routers/forecast.py}) follows a strict contract:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
    \item Load features from configured path.
    \item Resolve model bundle by explicit or fallback target mapping.
    \item Emit next-horizon forecast outputs with metadata.
    \item Surface missing-model states explicitly (not silent fallback fabrication).
\end{enumerate}
Resolution behavior is deterministic: explicit per-target model paths are checked first, then ordered fallback search (\texttt{lstm}, \texttt{tcn}, \texttt{gbm}) is applied under \path{artifacts/models}. If \texttt{ensemble\_models} is present in a GBM bundle, inference averages member predictions; quantiles are emitted from explicit quantile models when present, otherwise from stored residual quantile offsets.

\subsection{Component interfaces (inputs/outputs)}
Conformal calibration at $\alpha = 0.10$ is implemented in the uncertainty module (\texttt{conformal.py}). For point forecast $\hat{y}$ and calibrated residual quantile $q$, interval bounds are:
\[
\text{Lower} = \hat{y} - q, \qquad \text{Upper} = \hat{y} + q.
\]
The implementation supports horizon-wise quantiles, rolling updates, and adaptive scaling behavior for online coverage control.
It combines two mechanisms: \texttt{ConformalInterval} for calibrated interval construction and \texttt{AdaptiveConformal} for bounded online alpha updates under miss events. Default code-level settings include \texttt{alpha = 0.10}, \texttt{rolling = true}, and \texttt{rolling\_window = 720}.

\subsection{Runtime modes (simulation, shadow, active)}
The deterministic optimizer in \path{src/gridpulse/optimizer/lp_dispatch.py} solves a mixed-integer linear formulation with:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item battery charge/discharge decisions,
    \item grid import,
    \item SOC dynamics and constraints,
    \item cost and carbon objective terms,
    \item degradation and peak penalties.
\end{itemize}
Representative objective form:
\[
\min \sum_t \left(c_t^{\text{energy}} G_t + c_t^{\text{carbon}} G_t + c^{\text{deg}} (P_t^{\text{ch}} + P_t^{\text{dis}})\right).
\]
Key implementation behaviors:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
    \item Piecewise battery efficiency regimes (\texttt{efficiency\_regime\_a}, \texttt{efficiency\_regime\_b}) with SOC split gating.
    \item Optional interval-aware risk binding via \texttt{risk.mode = worst\_case\_interval}.
    \item High unmet-load and curtailment penalties to avoid unsafe economic shortcuts.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Artifact-locked reproducibility workflow}
DC3S is implemented in \path{src/gridpulse/dc3s/} and exposed through \path{POST /dc3s/step} and \path{GET /dc3s/audit/\{command\_id\}} in \path{services/api/routers/dc3s.py}. It composes telemetry quality weighting, drift detection, uncertainty inflation, safe action repair, and certificate-grade audit persistence.

The implemented interval inflation law is linear and bounded:
\[
\text{infl}=\mathrm{clip}\left(1+k_q(1-w_t)+k_d\mathbf{1}[\text{drift}],\,1,\,\text{infl}_{\max}\right)
\]
\[
\text{Lower}=\hat{y}-q\cdot \text{infl},\qquad \text{Upper}=\hat{y}+q\cdot \text{infl}
\]
\[
w_t \leftarrow \max(w_t,\text{min}_w)
\]

The reliability module (\path{quality.py}) computes $w_t$ from missingness, delay, out-of-order behavior, and spike penalties. Drift logic (\path{drift.py}) uses Page-Hinkley updates on residual magnitude $r_t = |y_t - \hat{y}_t|$ with configured warmup and cooldown controls.

Safety shielding (\path{shield.py}) supports two modes:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \texttt{projection}: deterministic clipping against SOC, power, and ramp constraints.
    \item \texttt{robust\_resolve}: attempt robust optimization under interval bounds, then project as final guard.
\end{enumerate}
Certification (\path{certificate.py}) computes \texttt{model\_hash} and \texttt{config\_hash}, supports optional \texttt{prev\_hash} chaining, and stores signed payload structure to DuckDB. Runtime online state (\path{state.py}) persists per \texttt{(zone, device, target)} keys including drift state, last inflation, latest telemetry/action context, and audit linkage pointers.

\subsection{RAC-Cert Unified Uncertainty-Control Method (Flagship)}
To convert interval construction from a fixed post-processing layer into a control-aware method class, this manuscript defines \textbf{RAC-Cert} (Reliability-Adaptive Conformal with Certified Robustness). RAC-Cert is implemented in \path{src/gridpulse/dc3s/rac_cert.py} and consumed by calibration/runtime paths in \path{src/gridpulse/dc3s/calibration.py} and \path{src/gridpulse/cpsbench_iot/runner.py}.

\subsubsection*{Notation and Calibration Objects}
At each time step $t$, let $\hat{y}_t$ denote point forecast, $q^{lo}_t$ and $q^{hi}_t$ denote base quantile forecasts, $w_t \in [0,1]$ denote telemetry reliability, and $s_t$ denote dispatch sensitivity.
On calibration index $i$, nonconformity is:
\[
\text{score}_i=\max\left(q^{lo}_i-y_i,\; y_i-q^{hi}_i,\;0\right).
\]
Calibration points are assigned to volatility bin $k$ using rolling volatility features (default three bins: low/mid/high). For each bin:
\[
\hat{q}_k=\text{Quantile}_{1-\alpha}\left(\text{score}_i\mid i\in k\right),\qquad
\hat{q}_{global}=\text{Quantile}_{1-\alpha}\left(\text{score}_i\right).
\]
To stabilize sparse bins, RAC-Cert applies shrinkage:
\[
\hat{q}^{shrunk}_k=\frac{n_k}{n_k+\tau}\hat{q}_k+\frac{\tau}{n_k+\tau}\hat{q}_{global},
\]
where $\tau$ is configurable (\texttt{qhat\_shrink\_tau}).

\subsubsection*{Reliability + Sensitivity Adaptive Width Law}
At runtime, RAC-Cert computes an effective half-width multiplier:
\[
m_t=1+\beta_r(1-w_t)+\beta_s \cdot \tilde{s}_t,
\]
with normalized sensitivity
\[
\tilde{s}_t=\mathrm{clip}\!\left(\frac{s_t}{s_{ref}},0,1\right).
\]
Effective conformal radius is:
\[
q^{eff}_t=\min(\hat{q}^{shrunk}_{k_t}\cdot m_t,\; q_{max}\cdot \hat{q}^{shrunk}_{k_t}),
\]
where $q_{max}$ is \texttt{max\_q\_multiplier}. Drift/reliability inflation remains explicit:
\[
\text{infl}_t=\mathrm{clip}\left(1+k_q(1-w_t)+k_d\mathbf{1}[\text{drift}]+k_s\tilde{s}_t,\;1,\;\text{infl}_{max}\right).
\]
Base RAC interval:
\[
L_t=\hat{y}_t-q^{eff}_t\cdot \text{infl}_t,\qquad U_t=\hat{y}_t+q^{eff}_t\cdot \text{infl}_t.
\]
Then ambiguity widening from \path{widen_bounds(...)} is applied as a final robustification stage.

\paragraph{Coverage statement (informal).}
The DC3S inflation rule is intended to tie per-step miscoverage to the effective reliability-adjusted level \(\delta \cdot w_t\), so low-reliability telemetry widens intervals rather than silently preserving nominal width. Informally, this means the method targets stricter uncertainty inflation when telemetry quality falls, while still relying on adaptive conformal logic for running-average coverage control rather than claiming a pointwise theorem for every step. This should be read as a method sketch, not a formal proof: the relevant background is adaptive/running-average conformal inference [Citation placeholder: Gibbs \& Cand\`es, JMLR 2024], weighted conformal ideas for non-uniform validity [Citation placeholder: Barber, Cand\`es, Ramdas, Tibshirani, Annals of Statistics 2023], and the subgaussian tail-bound intuition used in the inflation heuristic [Citation placeholder: Vershynin, 2018].

\subsection{CVaR formulation as risk-aware baseline}
CVaR is expressed via Rockafellar--Uryasev linearization:
\[
\min \ \eta + \frac{1}{(1-\beta)S}\sum_{s=1}^{S} u_s,
\qquad
u_s \ge C_s-\eta,\ \ u_s \ge 0.
\]
Before approving a numeric edit:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
    \item Confirm source artifact path exists and is versioned.
    \item Confirm row and field selection rule is unambiguous.
    \item Regenerate display values from raw values using manifest rounding rules.
    \item Verify metric synchronization in abstract, results, and conclusion.
    \item Re-run validator and archive pass output in revision notes.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Computational complexity and solver configuration}
Deterministic LP has the lowest runtime footprint; robust and CVaR variants increase variable/constraint counts through scenario dimensions and interval coupling. Canonical publication runs pin solver family, scenario counts, and seed list; staged short-horizon runs are used for correctness checks before full artifact regeneration.

\subsection{Long-Form Intro Block}
GridPulse is designed around a decision-first interpretation of machine learning in energy systems. Rather than ending at point prediction, the system carries uncertainty information into optimization, enforces dispatch feasibility under battery and grid constraints, and evaluates outcomes against explicit baselines. This architecture creates a measurable bridge between model behavior and operational value.

\subsection{Long-Form Method Block}
The forecasting layer trains LightGBM, LSTM, and TCN families under time-aware configurations with a 24-hour horizon and 168-hour lookback. Uncertainty is represented using conformal intervals with adaptive behavior. Deterministic optimization uses a mixed-integer structure with cost, carbon, degradation, curtailment, unmet-load, and peak terms. Robust optimization uses a two-scenario epigraph formulation over lower and upper load bounds, and safety checks are enforced before dispatch control actions are accepted.

\subsection{Long-Form Results Block}
Under the locked artifact policy, DE impact is 7.11\% cost savings, 0.30\% carbon reduction, and 6.13\% peak shaving. US impact is 0.11\% cost savings, 0.13\% carbon reduction, and 0.00\% peak shaving. Canonical stochastic evidence is tied to DE run \texttt{20260217\_165756} and US run \texttt{20260217\_182305}; both runs are feasible in run-summary outputs. Positive VSS appears in both regions with substantially different magnitudes.

\subsection{Long-Form Governance Block}
The manuscript uses a source-of-truth contract where publication-facing numeric claims must map to \path{paper/metrics_manifest.json} and corresponding entries in \path{paper/claim_matrix.csv}. Validation is automated via \path{scripts/validate_paper_claims.py}, which enforces required canonical strings and blocks known legacy contradictions.

\subsection{Long-Form Limitation Block}
Evidence is intentionally restricted to locked DE and US windows from dashboard profile artifacts. This improves traceability but limits direct external generalization. Optimization outcomes also depend on proxy price/carbon assumptions and scenario/interval settings that may vary across market designs.
