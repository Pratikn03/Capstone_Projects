\chapter{Simulated 15-Reviewer Audit Protocol}
\label{app:four-reviewer-audit}

This appendix records the canonical simulated review program for the live
ORIUS defense package.  The review is executed in three waves of five
reviewer lenses each.  Its job is not to declare theorems ``proved'' by
committee vote; its job is to pressure-test the defended surface against
logic drift, code/prose mismatch, scope inflation, and artifact drift.

The defended package is the active Battery + AV + Healthcare lane plus the
registry-canonical theorem-core split:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{flagship defended}: T1, T2, T3a, T4, T11, T\_trajectory\_PAC,
\item \textbf{supporting defended}: T3b, T6, T8, T11\textsubscript{Byz}, T\textsubscript{stale},
\item \textbf{draft / non-defended}: all remaining extension or open theorem surfaces.
\end{itemize}

All findings must terminate in exactly one disposition:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{fixed in code/tests}: the executable surface was corrected,
\item \textbf{narrowed in prose/registers}: the claim was reduced to match repo truth, or
\item \textbf{left open as bounded future work}: the claim remains outside the defended surface.
\end{itemize}

\section{Final Disposition Summary}

The simulated 15-reviewer audit does not certify that every theorem is
fully rigorous.  It certifies something narrower and more useful:
\begin{enumerate}
\item the canonical defense package is built on the repo-truthful path
  (\texttt{make review-compile}),
\item proof rigor is tracked by the active theorem audit rather than by a
  traceability gate pretending to be a proof checker,
\item extension laws and depth theorems are included only with their
  current bounded posture, and
\item any row that still lacks code, tests, register alignment, and proof
  alignment is explicitly narrowed or left open.
\end{enumerate}

That is the defended posture of the live repository after hardening: a
bounded, auditable theorem-and-artifact surface rather than a rhetorically
closed one.
